How and why public money is spent

Our goal is to promote the ability to understand a set of data, to learn to question the legitimacy of its source, and see the context in which these numbers are presented. We have chosen the UK Government public expenditure data for demonstration.

Public expenditure is defined as "money spent by the Government on public services and other state-controlled operations, projects and investments." Source

If public expenditure data is easily accessible, we will understand how our money is spent, and question if it was used wisely. However, people need good quality, well presented financial information to hold Government to account. Financial information requires a good understanding of accounts, therefore, we need to maximize transparency of spending purposes.

Given a set of accounts, we should be able to see what is available and visualize what interests us most. When we are able to place sections of public spending in context, we gain an understanding of the entire picture.
